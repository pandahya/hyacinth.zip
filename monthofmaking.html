One of my goals for the New Year is to make more.


I ended 2024 feeling burnt out and uninspired. I want to get back into creating, not only for my portfolio, but to bring more meaning and routine in my life through a creative endeavor.


For 2025, I've decided to spend each month of the year practicing a different craft.

I first learned art by sketching. My elementary school art teacher taught us to draw from pictures of objects, eventually moving on to still lives of things around us. When I began planning this, sketching immediately came to mind. I havenâ€™t seriously sat down with just a pencil and paper in years; I wanted to start the year off going back to basics.


I'd bought a tear off calendar from Muji for the new year. While contemplating what to do with the torn off pages, I found it to be the perfect medium for a daily sketch. With a new page every day, the date stamped cleanly and unchangeably, I have exactly twenty four hours time with it before it loses value.

Poster design was one of the first things I started with when learning graphic design, but it always felt like something I needed to practice more. To make this more digestible, I focused on the idea of event posters. I want to create a poster about something that happens every day, no matter the size of the "event".


My hope for this is to a) practice graphic design in a meaningful way and b) try to break from the routine mindset of a monotonous daily life. By making myself notice the smaller happenings, I can hopefully live a more present life and be more appreciative of more intimiate, fleeting moments.
